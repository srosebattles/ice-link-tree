Federal Immigration Operations in the Twin Cities

A collection of news coverage and documented incidents related to Operation Metro Surge (December 2025–present)

Last updated: January 16, 2026

Background Information

In early December 2025, the Department of Homeland Security launched "Operation Metro Surge," deploying federal agents to the Twin Cities metropolitan area. Officials have described it as the largest immigration enforcement operation in U.S. history. As of mid-January 2026, approximately 2,800 federal immigration agents are operating in the Twin Cities.

For comparison: the Minneapolis Police Department has approximately 600 officer positions. In July 2021, before the U.S. withdrawal from Afghanistan began, there were approximately 2,500 American troops in the country.

The Renee Good Shooting

On January 7, 2026, ICE agent Jonathan Ross shot and killed 37-year-old Renee Nicole Good, a U.S. citizen and mother of three, during an enforcement operation in a Minneapolis residential neighborhood. The Department of Homeland Security has called Good a "domestic terrorist" who attempted to ram agents with her vehicle. Local officials have disputed this characterization.
